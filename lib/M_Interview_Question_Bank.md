# M Interview Question Bank
Part 1–4: Behavioral · Product Design · Analytical · Strategy
97 Questions

---

## Part 1: Behavioral & Experience (22 Questions)

### 1.1 Decision-Making Under Uncertainty
**Q1** Tell me about a time you had to make a product decision with incomplete data. What did you decide, and what would have changed your mind?
**Q2** Describe a time you killed a feature or project that your team had already invested significant effort in. How did you make that call?
**Q3** Tell me about a time you launched something that underperformed. What did you learn, and what would you do differently?

### 1.2 Prioritization & Trade-offs
**Q4** Your engineering team has capacity for one project this quarter. Option A improves retention by an estimated 5% but takes the full quarter. Option B ships three smaller features that Sales has been requesting for months. Walk me through how you decide.
**Q5** You have three bugs reported this morning: one affects 1% of users but causes data loss, one affects 30% of users but is cosmetic, and one blocks a partnership launch next week. You can only fix two today. Which two and why?
**Q6** Tell me about a time you said no to a stakeholder who outranked you. What was the ask, why did you push back, and what happened?

### 1.3 Cross-Functional Collaboration
**Q7** Tell me about a time you and your engineering lead disagreed on the scope of a project. How did you resolve it, and were you happy with the outcome?
**Q8** Describe a situation where a design decision conflicted with what the data was telling you. What did you do?

### 1.4 Influence Without Authority
**Q9** Tell me about a time you needed to get buy-in from a team that didn't report to you and had no incentive to help. How did you do it?
**Q10** You're a new PM joining a team that's been running without a PM for six months. Engineers have been setting their own priorities. How do you earn trust without disrupting their momentum?

### 1.5 Ownership & Accountability
**Q11** Tell me about a project where the outcome was a failure and you were the one accountable. Not your team, not circumstances — you. What was your mistake?
**Q12** Describe a time you inherited someone else's mess — a broken feature, a mismanaged project, a toxic team dynamic. What did you do in the first two weeks?
**Q13** Tell me about a time you were wrong about a user need. How did you find out, and what did you do with that information?

### 1.6 Speed vs. Quality
**Q14** Describe a time you shipped something you knew wasn't perfect because speed mattered more. How did you decide what "good enough" meant?
**Q15** Tell me about a time you chose to slow down a project when the rest of the team wanted to move fast. What did you see that they didn't?
**Q16** You're two days from launch and your designer wants to redo the onboarding flow. Engineering says it's a one-week delay. The current flow tests fine but isn't great. What do you do?

### 1.7 Managing Up & Sideways
**Q17** Tell me about a time your manager gave you a directive you disagreed with. How did you handle it?
**Q18** Describe a situation where you had to deliver bad news to leadership — a missed deadline, a failed experiment, a wrong bet. How did you frame it?
**Q19** You discover that a peer PM's project is going to negatively impact your product's key metric. They don't realize it. What do you do?

### 1.8 Ambiguity & Scrappiness
**Q20** Tell me about a time you started a project with zero documentation, no clear requirements, and a vague mandate. How did you create clarity?
**Q21** Describe a time you had to validate a product idea with no budget for user research. What did you do?
**Q22** You join a new team and realize the product roadmap was built on assumptions no one has validated. What do you do?

## Part 2: Product Design & Sense (24 Questions)

### 2.1 Design From Scratch
**Q23** Design a feature that helps first-time hosts set their pricing on a marketplace platform. You have one engineer and four weeks.
**Q24** Help small businesses create their first ad in under 5 minutes. Design the onboarding flow for a user who has never run a digital ad before.
**Q25** Design a grocery delivery app for elderly users who are not comfortable with smartphones. Top three design decisions.
**Q26** Design a feature for a maps product that helps tourists decide where to eat in a city they've never been to. You cannot use ratings or reviews.
**Q27** Design a job search experience that works for people who are currently employed and don't want their employer to know they're looking.
**Q28** Design a feature that helps a group of 5 friends choose a playlist for a road trip when everyone has different taste.
**Q29** Design a checkout experience for an e-commerce app where 40% of users abandon at the payment step. You can change one thing. What is it?

### 2.2 Improve an Existing Product
**Q30** Pick a product you use daily that frustrates you. What's the single highest-impact change you'd make, and how would you measure if it worked?
**Q31** Short-video engagement is flat quarter over quarter. You can either improve the recommendation algorithm or redesign the creation tools. You can only invest in one this half. Which one?
**Q32** Watch time is up 10% but creator uploads are down 15% on a video platform. What's happening, and what would you do?
**Q33** A threading feature is widely used but widely disliked. Users say threads feel like a "black hole." How would you fix threads without removing them?
**Q34** A calendar product has hundreds of millions of users but most only use it for meetings. Design a feature that makes it useful for personal lives.
**Q35** A "Continue Watching" row is the most-used feature but users say it feels like a guilt trip. How would you redesign it?
**Q36** ETAs for a service app are wrong 30% of the time but users trust it more than competitors. Why might that be, and should you fix accuracy or preserve perception?

### 2.3 Product Trade-offs
**Q37** Users want end-to-end encryption in a messaging product, but trust & safety says it will make harassment detection nearly impossible. How do you think about this trade-off?
**Q38** Your e-commerce app can show "most popular" products (higher conversion) or "most relevant" products (higher satisfaction, lower immediate conversion). Which do you default to?
**Q39** User research says people love a proposed social feed. An A/B test shows engagement drops 20%. Ship or kill?

### 2.4 User Empathy, Edge Cases & Sensitive Design
**Q40** You're building scheduled messages for a messaging app. What's the most important edge case to design for?
**Q41** A ride-sharing app wants a "quiet ride" option. How do you design it without making drivers feel rated on personality?
**Q42** Design a feature that reduces negative mental health impact on teenagers without significantly reducing engagement.
**Q43** Your health tracking app shows a user's data trend that suggests a serious condition. How do you surface this information responsibly?

### 2.5 Simplification & Removal
**Q44** Your product has 12 features. 3 used by 90% of users, 5 by 20%, 4 by less than 3%. How do you decide what to remove, and how do you handle the 3% who rely on a low-usage feature?
**Q45** You're redesigning a settings page that has 47 options. Goal: reduce to 10 without user backlash. Walk me through your approach.
**Q46** Pick any widely-used product and identify one feature you would cut entirely. Defend your choice.

## Part 3: Analytical & Execution (28 Questions)

### 3.1 Metrics Definition & Selection
**Q47** You just launched an AI tool. It's been live one month. What's the single most important metric you're watching, and why that one over everything else?
**Q48** Your north star is DAU. A new feature increases DAU by 8% but decreases revenue per user by 3%. Is this a win?
**Q49** You're choosing between conversion rate and revenue per session for a new checkout flow. They point in different directions. Which do you optimize for, and when would you switch?
**Q50** Leadership wants "time spent" as the north star metric. You disagree. What metric would you propose instead, and why?
**Q51** Your team is launching a trust & safety feature. How do you measure success when the ideal outcome is something not happening?
**Q52** Power users are 5% of your base but generate 50% of revenue. Casual users are the other 95%. Leadership wants one unified metric. Is that possible, and is it a good idea?
**Q53** Your experiment's guardrail metric (support tickets) crossed the threshold, but your primary metric (conversion) improved significantly. Do you ship?

### 3.2 Root Cause Analysis & Data Interpretation
**Q54** Your app's DAU dropped 8% week over week. What's your first question before looking at any dashboard?
**Q55** Cancellation rate jumped 15% in one city. Supply unchanged. Prices unchanged. Top three hypotheses?
**Q56** Free-to-paid conversion rate went from 3% to 5%, but total paid users decreased. How is this possible, and what does it tell you?
**Q57** Average order value is up 20%, total orders are down 10%. Good or bad?
**Q58** Your NPS is 72 (excellent) but monthly churn is 8% (terrible). How do both coexist?
**Q59** After a new release, crash rate dropped from 2% to 0.5% — but user complaints about stability increased. What's going on?
**Q60** Searches for "return policy" spiked 300% this week. No policy changes were made. What do you investigate?
**Q61** Average transaction value has been increasing steadily for 6 months. Name three scenarios where this is actually a warning sign.
**Q62** An A/B test shows +3% overall, but desktop shows +8% and mobile shows -2%. What do you do?

### 3.3 Estimation, Sizing & Prioritization
**Q63** How many coffee shops in the US could benefit from an AI playlist product? Walk me through your estimation.
**Q64** Build in-house (8 weeks, 3 engineers) or buy third-party ($50K/year). How do you frame this decision?
**Q65** You have 30 feature requests and engineering can ship 5 this quarter. No stakeholder will deprioritize. How do you choose the 5 and say no to the 25?
**Q66** How do you think about the right mix of revenue features, tech debt, and UX improvements on a roadmap? Does the ratio change over time?
**Q67** A high-profile customer threatens to churn unless you build their feature in 4 weeks. Building it derails your sprint. How do you handle this?

### 3.4 Experimentation & Launch
**Q68** QA found a bug affecting 2% of users in a non-critical flow. Fixing it delays launch two weeks. Ship or delay?
**Q69** An A/B test shows 2% CTR improvement, but complaints in the test group tripled. What do you do?
**Q70** You shipped a feature and engagement is up 12%, but support tickets are up 40%. Keep, iterate, or roll back?
**Q71** You want to run an A/B test but only have 500 DAU. How do you validate your hypothesis?
**Q72** An experiment shows neutral results after 4 weeks. Your team wants to end it. Should you?
**Q73** During a phased rollout, everything is fine at 10%, but performance degrades at 50%. What's your immediate action?
**Q74** A feature is performing well in the US but poorly in Japan. Same product, same code. What do you investigate?

## Part 4: Strategy & Vision (23 Questions)

### 4.1 Market Entry & Expansion
**Q75** You're choosing between launching in a market with the most users but lowest average order value vs. a smaller market with 3x higher AOV. Which do you launch first, and why?
**Q76** Your B2B SaaS dominates US mid-market. Expand upmarket to enterprise or geographically to a new region? Each takes a year. How do you decide?

### 4.2 Competitive Response
**Q77** A competitor just launched a feature your team could copy in 8 weeks. Should you? What's your decision framework?
**Q78** A competitor dropped fees to zero, VC-subsidized, and is gaining share fast. What's your response?
**Q79** You run a profitable but slow-growing product. A new competitor is growing 5x faster by undercutting on price. What do you do?

### 4.3 Platform & Ecosystem
**Q80** Should a platform build its own payment system or continue using third-party providers? How do you evaluate this trade-off?
**Q81** You have hundreds of millions of users, most on a free tier. Do you push to convert free users to premium, or build new revenue streams for free users?
**Q82** A startup wants to build on your API. They're small now but could be a competitor in 3 years. Do you let them on the platform?
**Q83** You're launching a first-party feature that competes directly with the most popular third-party app in your ecosystem. How do you handle this?
**Q84** A major distribution partner wants a feature only their users see. It's a reasonable feature but sets a customization precedent. Do you build it?

### 4.4 Business Model & Monetization
**Q85** Your core product is free and ad-supported. The CEO asks you to explore subscriptions. What's the first thing you need to figure out before designing pricing?
**Q86** Your B2C app has 50M users but monetizes poorly. A PE firm suggests cutting features to improve margins. What do you advise the CEO?

### 4.5 Product Vision & Roadmap
**Q87** AI generates content in your product category at near-zero cost. Existential threat or opportunity? What do you build in the next 18 months?
**Q88** Your product is #3 in a market dominated by two incumbents. You can't outspend them. What's your 18-month strategy?
**Q89** Two roadmap options: (A) 6 incremental improvements with 15% expected revenue uplift, or (B) one big bet with a 30% chance of 100% uplift but 70% chance of zero. Which do you choose and why?
**Q90** Your team has been building a feature for 4 months. New data suggests the problem doesn't exist at scale. The feature is 80% done. What do you do?

### 4.6 Long-term Bets & Disruption
**Q91** What's the biggest threat to your target company's core business in the next five years? What would you build today to address it?
**Q92** New regulation requires showing users screen time and nudging breaks. Engagement will drop 10–15%. How do you pitch this to leadership as an opportunity?
**Q93** Voice interfaces have been "the next big thing" for 10 years. What would need to change for voice to become primary? Would you bet on it?
**Q94** AR/VR headsets are improving fast but adoption is niche. When is the right time to invest seriously? How do you know if you're too early?
**Q95** Autonomous technology is transforming a core industry. If you're PM at your target company, what do you build in the next 5 years to stay ahead?
**Q96** A major tech platform approaches your company wanting to integrate your product into their ecosystem. They offer 100M users overnight but control the UX and take 30% of revenue. Do you accept?
**Q97** A large acquirer offers a premium to buy your product. Your team wants to stay independent. How do you think through this decision?
