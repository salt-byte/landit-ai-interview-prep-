## 1. Company-Specific Evaluation Rubrics

### Google

**4 Core Evaluation Attributes (scored by every interviewer):**

| Attribute | What It Measures |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **General Cognitive Ability (GCA)** | Problem-solving, analytical thinking, ability to break down ambiguous problems logically |
| **Role-Related Knowledge (RRK)** | PM-specific competencies: product lifecycle, prioritization, technical depth, metrics, crisis management |
| **Leadership** | Cross-functional influence, driving decisions without authority, rallying teams around a vision |
| **Googleyness** | Cultural fit — comfort with ambiguity, collaborative spirit, bias toward action, intellectual humility |

**Interview Loop Structure:** 4-6 interviews (30-45 min each) with PMs, engineers, and ML engineers (for AI roles).

**5 Performance Levels** (per Lewis C. Lin):
- Each dimension is rated across 5 tiers from "poor" to "exceptional"
- Interviewers assess product design, analytical thinking, and technical depth separately
- Each interviewer submits detailed feedback across ALL four attributes before the hiring committee reviews

**What Google Interviewers Write on Scorecards:**
- Specific examples from the candidate's answers
- Strengths/weaknesses tied to each of the 4 attributes
- Overall hire recommendation
- Hiring committee makes final decision (not individual interviewers)

### Meta (Facebook)

**3 Core Interview Themes / Evaluation Dimensions:**

| Round | What It Tests | Key Evaluation Criteria |
|-------|--------------|----------------------|
| **Product Sense** (2 rounds) | User empathy, design thinking, product vision | Can they identify who to build for? Do they make intentional design choices? Can they handle constraints and critique? |
| **Analytical Thinking / Execution** (2 rounds) | Data-driven decision making | Goals & metrics setting, problem debugging, trade-off analysis, prioritization |
| **Leadership & Drive** (1 round) | Ownership, resilience, influence | Trust-building, learning from experience, team support, execution ability |

**Scoring Scale (5 ratings):**
- Below Expectations
- Meets Expectations
- Strong
- Exceptional
- Unable to Evaluate

**Plus summary recommendations:** "Soft no," "Suggest re-evaluate," "Strong yes"

**Key Rule:** Interviewers submit feedback within 24 hours and CANNOT see other evaluators' submissions before completing their own (prevents anchoring bias).

**Recent Addition — AI Product Sense Round:**
- Tests application of AI to solve user problems
- Evaluates when AI solutions make strategic sense vs. when they don't

### Amazon

**Evaluation Framework: 16 Leadership Principles**

Each interviewer is assigned **2-3 specific Leadership Principles** to probe. They rate candidates as:
- **Below the bar** — Does not meet standards
- **Meets the bar** — Acceptable performance
- **Raises the bar** — Exceeds current team quality

**Most Critical LPs for PM Roles:**

| Leadership Principle | What It Tests |
|---------------------|--------------|
| **Customer Obsession** | Starts with the customer and works backward |
| **Ownership** | Thinks long-term, acts on behalf of the entire company |
| **Bias for Action** | Speed matters; calculated risk-taking |
| **Think Big** | Bold vision, not incremental thinking |
| **Invent and Simplify** | Innovation and simplification |
| **Dive Deep** | Attention to detail, data-driven decisions |
| **Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit** | Conviction + ability to commit once decided |
| **Are Right, A Lot** | Good judgment and instincts |
| **Insist on the Highest Standards** | Quality bar |

**Bar Raiser System:**
- One interviewer is a trained "Bar Raiser" — NOT from the hiring team
- Has veto power over any candidate
- Ensures hiring quality remains high regardless of team urgency
- Specifically tests "Are Right, A Lot" and "Insists on the Highest Standards"

**Interview Types for PM:**
- 2 product design-focused interviews (Think Big, Invent and Simplify, Dive Deep)
- 2-3 behavioral interviews (using STAR method against assigned LPs)
- Technical assessment (system design / working backward from customer)

### TikTok / ByteDance

**Interview Structure:** Up to 6 rounds, progressing from IC PMs to VP-level interviewers.

**Core Evaluation Dimensions:**

| Dimension | What They Look For |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **Product Sense** | User-focused thinking, ability to define success metrics, understanding of content creation/consumption dynamics |
| **Strategy** | Competitive positioning, creator vs. viewer dynamics, content monetization, algorithm/FYP understanding |
| **Analytical** | Data-driven decision-making, metric comprehension |
| **Technical** | ML fundamentals, recommendation systems, engineering collaboration (NOT coding) |
| **Behavioral** | Cross-functional collaboration, clear communication, customer obsession, growth mindset |

**Unique TikTok Criteria:**
- Deep understanding of recommendation systems and For You Page algorithm
- Knowledge of creator vs. viewer behavior dynamics
- Content strategy and user-generated content platform experience
- Ability to communicate across language/cultural barriers (global product)

## 2. Common PM Interview Frameworks

### Books & Their Core Frameworks

| Book | Author | Key Frameworks |
|------|--------|---------------|
| **Decode and Conquer** (5th ed.) | Lewis C. Lin | CIRCLES Method, AARM Method, DIGS Method |
| **Cracking the PM Interview** | Gayle McDowell & Jackie Bavaro | Covers estimation, behavioral, product, technical, and pitch questions |
| **Swipe to Unlock** | Mehta, Detroja, Agashe | Technical concepts for non-engineers |

### Key Frameworks in Detail

**CIRCLES Method (Product Design Questions):**
- **C**omprehend the situation
- **I**dentify the customer
- **R**eport the customer's needs
- **C**ut, through prioritization
- **L**ist solutions
- **E**valuate tradeoffs
- **S**ummarize recommendation

Google recruiters officially recommend CIRCLES as the #1 method for product design questions.

**AARM Method (Metrics Questions):**
- **A**cquisition — How users find the product
- **A**ctivation — First-time user experience
- **R**etention — Users coming back
- **M**onetization — Revenue generation

**DIGS Method (Behavioral Questions):**
- **D**ramatize the situation
- **I**ndicate the alternatives
- **G**o through what you did
- **S**ummarize the impact

**GAME Method (Metric Definition):**
- **G**oals — Business objectives
- **A**ctions — Key user actions
- **M**etrics — Specific measurable KPIs
- **E**valuation — How to interpret results

**Additional Frameworks Used in PM Interviews:**
- RICE (Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort) — for prioritization
- HEART (Happiness, Engagement, Adoption, Retention, Task success) — for UX metrics
- Porter's Five Forces — for strategy questions
- SWOT — for competitive analysis
- 5Cs — for market environment overview
- SPSIL (Situation, Problem, Solution, Impact, Lessons) — for behavioral answers

## 3. Universal Scoring Dimensions

Across all top tech companies, PM interviewers consistently evaluate these core competencies:

### The 4 Primary Attributes (Industry Standard)

| Dimension | Score Range | What "Strong" Looks Like |
|-----------|-----------|-------------------------|
| **Product Sense / Intuition** | 1-3 or 1-5 | Identifies real user problems, proposes creative solutions, demonstrates genuine empathy for users, makes thoughtful tradeoffs |
| **Analytical / Execution** | 1-3 or 1-5 | Defines meaningful metrics, structures problems logically, reasons with data, debugs issues systematically |
| **Leadership / Communication** | 1-3 or 1-5 | Influences without authority, communicates clearly and concisely, drives decisions, shows resilience |
| **Cultural Fit / Values** | 1-3 or 1-5 | Aligns with company values, collaborative, comfortable with ambiguity, growth mindset |

### Scoring Principles Interviewers Follow

1. **"If it's not an easy yes, it's an easy no"** — When torn between scores, default to the lower rating
2. **Process over outcome** — Judge the candidate's thought process and structure, NOT whether they arrive at a "correct" specific answer
3. **Independent evaluation** — Each interviewer scores independently before seeing others' feedback
4. **Specific evidence required** — Interviewers must cite specific examples from the interview to justify their score
5. **Calibrated scoring** — Companies train interviewers to calibrate what "meets bar" vs "exceeds bar" looks like

### What Separates Strong from Weak Candidates

| Criteria | Weak (1) | Acceptable (2) | Strong (3) |
|----------|----------|----------------|------------|
| **Structure** | Rambling, no framework | Some structure, inconsistent | Clear framework, logical flow |
| **User Focus** | Generic personas, no empathy | Identifies users but surface-level | Deep user segmentation, real pain points |
| **Prioritization** | No rationale for choices | Some criteria but weak justification | Clear criteria (impact, effort, strategic fit), defensible |
| **Metrics** | Vague or wrong metrics | Reasonable metrics, no hierarchy | North star + supporting metrics, explains why each matters |
| **Communication** | Unclear, verbose, unfocused | Clear but not compelling | Concise, persuasive, adapts to audience |
| **Depth** | Surface-level answers | Good breadth, limited depth | Can go deep on any dimension when probed |

## 4. Question Types and What They Evaluate

### The 11 PM Interview Question Types

| # | Question Type | What It Evaluates | Example Question | Framework to Use |
|---|--------------|-------------------|------------------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Product Design** | Product sense, user empathy, creativity | "Design a product for elderly people to stay connected with family" | CIRCLES |
| 2 | **Product Improvement** | Analytical thinking, user understanding | "How would you improve Google Maps?" | Identify users → pain points → solutions → prioritize |
| 3 | **Favorite Product** | Industry knowledge, critical thinking, passion | "What's your favorite product and why?" | Structure: what, who, why, improvement |
| 4 | **Metric Definition** | KPI selection, business acumen | "What metrics would you track for YouTube Shorts?" | GAME, AARM |
| 5 | **Metric Change / Root Cause** | Debugging, structured analysis | "Uber rides dropped 10% last week. Why?" | Internal → external → user behavior tree |
| 6 | **Strategy** | Competitive thinking, long-term vision | "Should Spotify enter the podcast market?" | Porter's Five Forces, SWOT |
| 7 | **Estimation / Market Sizing** | Logical reasoning, math under pressure | "How many iPhones are sold in the US per year?" | Top-down or bottom-up + sense-check |
| 8 | **Prioritization & Tradeoffs** | Decision-making, stakeholder management | "You have 3 features but can only build 1. How do you decide?" | RICE, impact vs effort |
| 9 | **Behavioral** | Leadership, collaboration, self-awareness | "Tell me about a time you disagreed with an engineer" | DIGS, SPSIL, STAR |
| 10 | **Technical** | Technical literacy, communication to non-tech audiences | "Explain how a recommendation algorithm works" | Simplify → analogy → layers of depth |
| 11 | **Generic / Fit** | Motivation, company knowledge, career alignment | "Why this company? Why PM?" | Company research + personal narrative |

### Which Companies Emphasize Which Question Types

| Company | Primary Focus | Secondary Focus | Unique Element |
|---------|--------------|----------------|---------------|
| **Google** | Product Design, Analytical, Strategy | Technical, Behavioral | Googleyness culture fit |
| **Meta** | Product Sense, Analytical Thinking | Leadership & Drive | AI Product Sense (new) |
| **Amazon** | Behavioral (LP-based), Product Design | Strategy, Technical | Bar Raiser veto system |
| **TikTok** | Strategy, Product Design | Technical (ML/RecSys), Analytical | Recommendation system knowledge |
| **Apple** | Product Design, Portfolio review | Technical depth, Strategy | Design-heavy, taste & craft |
| **Microsoft** | Behavioral, Product Design | Strategy, Technical | Collaboration & growth mindset |

## 5. Practical Scorecard Structure

### What an Actual PM Interview Scorecard Looks Like

After each interview round, the interviewer fills out a form similar to this:

```
CANDIDATE: _______________     ROLE: PM, L5
INTERVIEWER: _____________     ROUND: Product Sense

OVERALL RECOMMENDATION:  [ ] Strong No  [ ] Lean No  [ ] Lean Yes  [ ] Strong Yes

DIMENSIONS (1-5 scale):

1. Problem Framing & User Understanding     ___/5
   Notes: [specific examples from interview]

2. Solution Quality & Creativity            ___/5
   Notes: [specific examples from interview]

3. Prioritization & Tradeoff Reasoning      ___/5
   Notes: [specific examples from interview]

4. Communication & Structure                ___/5
   Notes: [specific examples from interview]

5. Technical Depth (if applicable)          ___/5
   Notes: [specific examples from interview]

STRENGTHS:
- [specific strength with evidence]

AREAS OF CONCERN:
- [specific concern for committee to explore]

ADDITIONAL NOTES FOR HIRING COMMITTEE:
- [anything else worth flagging]
```

### How Hiring Decisions Are Made

| Company | Decision Process |
|---------|-----------------|
| **Google** | Hiring committee (not interviewers) reviews all scorecards + resume + references. Interviewers don't make the call. |
| **Meta** | Interviewers submit independent feedback. Hiring manager + recruiter review aggregate scores. |
| **Amazon** | All interviewers debrief together. Bar Raiser has veto power. Consensus-driven but Bar Raiser-guarded. |
| **TikTok** | Progressive rounds with increasing seniority. VP-level makes final call. |

## Key Takeaways for Interview Prep

1. **Every company scores on 3-4 core dimensions:** Product Sense, Analytical/Execution, Leadership, and Cultural Fit
2. **Structure matters more than "right answers"** — interviewers evaluate your thinking process
3. **Use named frameworks** (CIRCLES, AARM, GAME) — they signal you know the language
4. **Specific evidence is required** — interviewers must justify scores with examples from your answers
5. **Independent scoring prevents groupthink** — each interviewer evaluates before seeing others' feedback
6. **The bar is "would I want to work with this person daily?"** — not just competence but collaboration quality
